Some Key Terms in Figure Skating

Click the arrow next to a term to read its definition.

Axel: A jump with a half turn in the air before the take-off. It is the only jump with a forward take-off.
Lutz: A jump with a toe pick on the opposite foot from the landing foot, followed by a full turn in the air.
Flip: A jump with a toe pick on the same foot as the landing foot, followed by a half turn in the air.
Loop: A jump with a toe pick on the same foot as the landing foot, followed by one and a half turns in the air.
Salchow: A jump with a toe pick on the back outside edge of one foot, followed by a full turn in the air.
Toe Loop A jump with a toe pick on the pick teeth of the blade, followed by a two and a half turns in the air
Camel Spin A spin performed on one knee with the other foot extended behind
Sit Spin A spin performed in a sitting position on the ice.
Upright Spin A spin performed on one foot with the other foot raised
Step Sequence: A series of steps and turns performed on the ice.
Edgework The use of the edges of the skate blades to grip the ice.
Spiral A glide on one foot with the other leg extended behind the body.
Biellman Spin A spin in which the skater grabs their skate blade behind their back.
